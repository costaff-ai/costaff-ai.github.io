02  Colour

One green, two neutrals.

The green does the recognising — it's the first thing anyone registers about the mark, which is why it stays a single flat value with no gradients. The wordmark stays pure black or pure white so it holds its contrast on any surface.

CoStaff Green #3A5032 The icon container, and the only brand colour.
Black #000000 The wordmark on light backgrounds.
White #FFFFFF The letters inside the mark, and the wordmark on dark backgrounds.
03  Spacing & size

Room to breathe, and a floor for legibility.

Logos get crowded easily — a headline or another mark sitting too close pulls the eye away and the shape stops registering. A consistent margin and a minimum size solve both problems.

04  Ways it works

Most of what you'd want to do is fine.

You don't need to ask us to write about CoStaff or show the logo. The things on the right aren't forbidden so much as self-defeating — they each chip away at the recognition the mark is there to provide.

Works well

  • Writing about CoStaffIn articles, tutorials, talks and slides — no permission needed.
  • Linking backUsing the logo as a link to this site or the GitHub repository.
  • Describing what you built“Built with CoStaff” or “compatible with CoStaff”.
  • Community materialMeetup slides, posters, community posts.
  • Scaling to fitAny size you like, as long as the proportions stay locked.

Weakens recognition

  • Changing the colourThe single green is the first thing people recognise.
  • Stretching or squashingThe proportions are part of what makes it readable.
  • Rotating or croppingThe lockup is drawn to sit level and whole.
  • Adding shadows or outlinesEffects muddy the silhouette, especially small.
  • Busy or same-toned backgroundsThe mark stops separating from what's behind it.
  • Rebuilding it yourselfRetyping the wordmark won't match the spacing — use the files.

Code and brand, briefly. CoStaff's source is open under AGPL v3 — fork it, ship it, build on it freely. The logo and name sit outside that licence, for a practical reason: they're how someone tells an official release from a fork. So if you're shipping your own thing built on CoStaff, give it its own name and mark, and say it's built with CoStaff — which is exactly what the guide above is here to help with.
